Research

About My Research

In my research I am interested in how animals look at the world and at the evolution of cognition. I use wild Tanganyikan cichlids — a spectacular adaptive radiation of freshwater fish — as a model system to ask comparative questions about cognitive abilities under natural ecological conditions.

By studying cognition in the wild, I aim to understand how and why cognitive abilities evolve, and how they are shaped by ecology, sociality, and individual experience.

I am also deeply interested in animal welfare and the ethics of using animals in research. I believe that rigorous science and compassionate treatment of animals are fully compatible goals.
