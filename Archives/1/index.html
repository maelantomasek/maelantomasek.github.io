About Me

I am a doctor in comparative psychology (PhD) and in veterinary medicine (DVM). I study animal behaviour and cognition, with a focus on how animals perceive their world and on the evolution of cognitive abilities.

My research centres on wild Tanganyikan cichlids as a model system for understanding the ecology and evolution of cognition. I am equally invested in animal welfare and the ethics of animal research.

I am also an author — my debut novel Le Plafond (The Ceiling) was published in 2025.

PhD candidate at LAPSCO, CNRS / Université Clermont Auvergne  &  Max Planck Institute of Animal Behaviour · Supervised by Valérie Dufour and Alex Jordan

Research

About My Research

In my research I am interested in how animals look at the world and at the evolution of cognition. I use wild Tanganyikan cichlids — a spectacular adaptive radiation of freshwater fish — as a model system to ask comparative questions about cognitive abilities under natural ecological conditions.

By studying cognition in the wild, I aim to understand how and why cognitive abilities evolve, and how they are shaped by ecology, sociality, and individual experience.

I am also deeply interested in animal welfare and the ethics of using animals in research. I believe that rigorous science and compassionate treatment of animals are fully compatible goals.

Science Communication

About My Science Communication

In addition to my research, I am very involved in science communication. I believe that sharing science with broad audiences is one of the most important things a researcher can do — both to educate people about science and to build empathy for the animals we study.

From podcasts and live events to social media and public talks, I try to bring fish cognition (and science more broadly) to as many people as possible, in France and beyond.

Books

About My Books

As an author, I am interested in linking science with imagination, to push the limits of what we know and to raise empathy. Combining my scientific background with storytelling, I write fiction that invites readers to question their assumptions about perception, difference, and the animal world.
